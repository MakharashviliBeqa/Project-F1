About F1

Formula 1 (F1) is the world’s premier motorsport, blending speed, precision, and cutting-edge technology to
create the most thrilling racing experience. Since its inception in 1950, F1 has captivated fans with high-
speed Grands Prix, legendary drivers like Ayrton Senna, Michael Schumacher, and Lewis Hamilton, and iconic
teams such as Ferrari, Mercedes, and Red Bull Racing.
Every F1 race is a fierce battle of speed, strategy, and engineering, with teams relentlessly pushing the
boundaries of innovation. F1 cars feature advanced hybrid power units, aerodynamic technologies, and
systems that influence not only the sport but also the future of automotive engineering.
F1’s global calendar includes races on some of the most famous circuits, from the glamorous Monaco Grand
Prix to the fast-paced Suzuka in Japan. These events unite millions of fans around the world, who share
the excitement of intense competition and the thrill of seeing the best drivers in the world compete for victory.
But F1 is more than just racing—it’s a test of human and technological excellence. It’s a sport that celebrates
the relentless pursuit of perfection, where every detail matters and every second counts. From engineering
marvels to unforgettable moments on track, Formula 1 offers an unparalleled experience that continues to inspire
fans and future generations of motorsport enthusiasts.
